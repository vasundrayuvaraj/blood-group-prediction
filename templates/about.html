About the Project

Abstract

This study highlights the uniqueness of fingerprints in identifying individuals, which remains unchanged throughout life. Fingerprint evidence is highly valued in legal contexts due to its distinctiveness, with a low likelihood of resemblance even among identical twins. At the same time, knowing blood is also a crucial to everyone while they are donating blood to others or else to take treatment. Currently, the manual tests conducted by lab phlebotomist in laboratories can be time-consuming and prone to human error, which can have severe consequences, particularly in urgent situations or blood donations. To address these challenges, we propose a non- invasive method of finding blood group which uses convolutional neural network (CNN). The CNN architecture, LeNet is used, which is a pre-trained model. During this project we committed to extract some features from the data, and with the help of that data, a model LeNet will be trained to predict blood group without taking blood sample (Non-Invasive method). Keywords: Blood group determination, fingerprint pattern, ridge frequency, LeNet, Convolutional neural networks

Introduction

The mostly used fingerprint application is identification of individuals using fingerprint patterns. Most of the organizations in India follows, fingerprint based biometric identification and also the gender and age identification is also considerable modality of the application. There are four sorts of fingerprints plans found in fingers are Loops, Whorls, Arches, and Mixed or Composites. The important characteristic feature fingerprint image may contain such as line connecting the features in different regions of image. Detecting blood groups using fingerprints is an innovative and non-invasive approach that combines biometric technology with medical information. This method leverages the unique patterns found in an individual's fingerprint to determine their blood type, which is an important piece of medical information for healthcare professionals

Advantages

This system offers several advantages. It helps rural populations by determining their blood group with minimal medical resources and without the need for trained experts, benefiting those who fear needles. It also aids blood donation camps by efficiently identifying blood groups. Additionally, our system provides blood transfusion compatibility services and personalized nutrition recommendations based on blood group, enhancing its practical utility and impact.

Limitations

  • Accuracy Concerns:

    The method may not achieve the same level of accuracy as traditional blood tests, potentially leading to misclassification of blood groups.

  • Data Variability:

    Fingerprint patterns can vary significantly among individuals, and establishing a consistent correlation with blood groups might be challenging task.

  • Technical Challenges:

    Developing a robust machine learning model requires extensive and high-quality data, which can be difficult to collect and process.

  • Cost and Accessibility of Technology:

    While the method aims to eliminate specialized equipment, the initial development and deployment of the technology might be costly and require advanced infrastructure.
